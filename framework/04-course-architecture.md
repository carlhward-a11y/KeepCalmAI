# 04. Course Architecture

## Purpose

This document defines how KeepCalmAI learning content is organised.

The platform should feel like a calm learning map rather than a long course.

## Learning hierarchy

KeepCalmAI uses this structure:

1. Platform
2. Collections
3. Journeys
4. Lessons
5. Steps

## Collections

Collections group content by broad learner need.

Initial collections:

- Foundations
- Everyday Life
- Work
- Study
- Creative
- Accessibility
- Business

## Journeys

A journey is a guided route through a topic.

Examples:

- AI Without Overwhelm
- AI for Everyday Life
- AI for Work Confidence
- Choosing Your First AI Assistant

A journey should have a clear outcome and visible progress.

## Lessons

A lesson explains one practical concept.

Each lesson is made of small steps and should normally take between 5 and 12 minutes to complete.

## Steps

A step is the smallest learning unit.

Each step should:

- Focus on one idea
- Take about two minutes or less
- Use plain language
- Include a useful visual where appropriate
- End with one clear action or continuation

## Progress

Learners should always know where they are.

Use wording such as:

- Step 2 of 6
- You have completed 3 discoveries
- Next: Try asking your first prompt

Avoid language that makes people feel tested, judged or behind.

## Optional routes

Not every learner needs every lesson.

Journeys may include optional routes for:

- ChatGPT
- Microsoft Copilot
- Google Gemini
- Claude
- Apple Intelligence
- Unsure or not chosen yet

## Confidence checkpoints

Confidence checkpoints are used instead of formal tests where possible.

Example question:

How confident do you feel about this?

- Still unsure
- Getting there
- Quite confident
- I could show someone else

If a learner is still unsure, offer another explanation, example or practice activity.
