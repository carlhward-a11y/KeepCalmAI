# 02. Learning Philosophy

KeepCalmAI teaches through small, practical and confidence-building steps.

The platform is designed for people who may feel unsure, excluded or overwhelmed by AI. The learning experience must therefore feel calm, predictable and manageable.

## Core principles

### People first

Technology is never the focus. Confidence is.

### Small wins

Learners should make visible progress every few minutes.

### One idea at a time

Each learning step should teach one concept only.

### Practical immediately

Every concept should connect to something the learner can try, use or recognise.

### Show before explain

Use simple visuals, examples, screenshots or demonstrations before relying on longer explanation.

### Explain jargon gently

Technical terms may be introduced, but only when useful and always with a plain English explanation.

### Confidence before coverage

It is better for a learner to understand three ideas clearly than to skim ten ideas and feel confused.

## The KeepCalm Method

Every lesson should follow this rhythm:

1. Calm: explain what is about to happen and reduce anxiety.
2. Learn: introduce one idea.
3. See: show the idea through a simple visual, example or demonstration.
4. Try: give the learner one practical action.
5. Reflect: help the learner notice what happened.
6. Confident: summarise the key takeaway and reinforce progress.

## Golden rules

### The 2-minute rule

No learning step should take more than about two minutes to complete. If it does, split it into smaller steps.

### The one idea rule

Every screen teaches one concept.

### The one action rule

Every page asks the learner to do one thing.

### The low-scroll rule

Where possible, each learning step should fit on one screen, especially on mobile.

### The progress rule

The learner should always know where they are in the journey.

### The small success rule

Every few minutes, the learner should experience a clear moment of progress.
