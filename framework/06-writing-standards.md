# 06. Writing Standards

## Purpose

KeepCalmAI writing should make AI feel understandable, useful and safe to try.

The tone should be calm, practical and human.

## Voice

Write as a patient guide, not a technical expert showing knowledge.

The writing should be:

- Clear
- Reassuring
- Practical
- Honest
- Respectful
- Friendly without being over-familiar

## Sentence style

Use short and medium-length sentences.

Avoid long explanations where a simple example would work better.

Use UK English.

## Paragraph length

Keep paragraphs short.

Most paragraphs should be one to three sentences.

## Jargon

Avoid jargon unless it is useful.

When a technical term is needed, explain it immediately in plain English.

Example:

A prompt is the instruction or question you give to an AI assistant.

## Preferred wording

Use:

- Try this
- Here is one way to do it
- You are still in control
- This is normal
- You can change your mind

Avoid:

- Simply
- Obviously
- Just
- Easy
- Master
- Hack
- Revolutionise

## Examples

Examples should feel realistic and useful.

Prefer everyday examples before technical ones.

## Safety and trust

Do not overstate what AI can do.

Remind learners that AI can make mistakes and that important information should be checked.

## Reading experience

A learner should never feel they are facing a wall of text.

Break content into short sections, clear headings, visuals and actions.
